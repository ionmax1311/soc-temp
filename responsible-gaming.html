You should always feel in control of your gaming. If you find yourself playing compulsively, feeling anxious when not playing, or using games to escape problems, it may be time to reassess.

Tips for Healthy Gaming Habits

01

Set Time Limits

Decide in advance how long you'll play and stick to it. Use timers or alarms to remind yourself when your session should end. Take regular breaks‚Äîideally, a 10-15 minute break every hour.

02

Never Chase Losses

Remember: our games use virtual credits only. If you're having an unlucky streak, don't try to "win it back." This mindset can lead to unhealthy patterns. Accept that randomness is part of the game and step away if you're feeling frustrated.

03

Keep Gaming Social

Engage with friends, family, and our community in healthy ways. Gaming should enhance your social life, not replace it. If you find yourself isolating to play, that's a red flag.

04

Prioritize Real Life

Your responsibilities, relationships, health, and wellbeing should always come first. If gaming starts interfering with sleep, work, school, or personal relationships, it's time to cut back.

05

Recognize Your Triggers

Be aware of emotional states that lead to excessive gaming. Are you playing to avoid stress, boredom, or difficult emotions? Find healthier coping mechanisms like exercise, hobbies, or talking to someone you trust.

06

Take Regular Breaks

Even if you're enjoying yourself, step away periodically. Stretch, hydrate, get fresh air, and give your eyes a rest from the screen. Physical activity helps maintain perspective and balance.

Support & Help Resources

If you or someone you know is struggling with gaming addiction or compulsive gaming behavior, help is available. These organizations provide free, confidential support:

Gamblers Anonymous

24/7 Support

A fellowship of men and women who share their experience, strength, and hope with each other to solve their common problem and help others recover from gambling addiction.

www.gamblersanonymous.org

National Council on Problem Gambling (NCPG)

Helpline Available

Provides resources, treatment referrals, and 24/7 confidential helpline support for individuals and families affected by problem gaming.

www.ncpgambling.org 1-800-522-4700 (Helpline)

Computer Gaming Addicts Anonymous (CGAA)

Peer Support

A fellowship for those who wish to stop compulsive computer gaming. Based on the 12-step recovery program, offering meetings and support worldwide.

cgaa.info

Self-Assessment Questions

Answer these questions honestly. If you answer "yes" to several of them, it may be time to seek support or make changes to your gaming habits:

  • Do you often play for longer than you intended?
  • Have you tried to cut back on gaming but been unable to?
  • Do you feel restless or irritable when you can't play?
  • Have you lied to family or friends about how much you play?
  • Has gaming interfered with your work, school, or responsibilities?
  • Do you game to escape problems or relieve negative feelings?
  • Have relationships suffered because of your gaming?
  • Do you neglect sleep, meals, or hygiene to keep playing?
  • Do you spend more time thinking about gaming than other activities?
  • Have you lost interest in hobbies or activities you used to enjoy?

If you answered "yes" to 3 or more questions, consider reaching out to one of the support resources listed above. There's no shame in seeking help‚Äîit's a sign of strength.
